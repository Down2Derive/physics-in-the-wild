Current Roles

Building the tools we need to uncover dark matter

I collaborate with multidisciplinary teams of scientists and engineers to design, simulate, and deploy systems that push the limits of sensitivity.

Research Assistant · nEXO Collaboration (2025–Present)

Advancing charge amplification components for the next-generation nEXO detector with SLAC engineers, iterating from CAD design through simulation and prototyping.

  • Modeled electrostatic behavior in COMSOL and SimIon to optimize signal yield.
  • Fabricated precision assemblies using Solidworks and Fusion 360 workflows.
  • Coordinated lab testing campaigns to validate performance against detector requirements.
SLAC COMSOL Fusion 360 C++

Data Science Researcher · LSSTC Fellowship (2024–2025)

Built a machine-learning pipeline to distinguish stars from galaxies in Rubin LSST imaging, enabling faster downstream cosmological analyses.

  • Engineered feature extraction routines and cross-validation strategies for large astronomical catalogs.
  • Delivered a reproducible workflow with Python, NumPy, and Scikit-learn optimized for scale.
  • Presented findings through interactive visualizations and the McNair Scholars research poster.
Python NumPy Scikit-learn Data Pipelines

Research Trainee · SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory (2024)

Developed Python tooling to process astronomical images for dark matter searches, focusing on noise characterization and enhanced detection of faint signals.

  • Automated calibration routines for imaging data captured in accelerator experiments.
  • Worked closely with physicists to interpret results and iterate on detection thresholds.
