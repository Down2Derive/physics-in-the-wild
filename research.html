Current Projects

Learning by pairing hardware intuition with data analysis

Each role helps me grow specific skills—from neutrino detector component studies to large-scale survey analysis and course-based investigations.

Summer Research Intern · nEXO Collaboration (SLAC, 2025)

Joined the nEXO detector R&D group as a McNair and Cal-Bridge scholar, focusing on PCB-based feedthrough studies for neutrinoless double beta decay instrumentation.

  • Practiced PCB layout, impedance matching, and micro-component soldering for prototype boards.
  • Modeled mounting hardware and component constraints in SolidWorks and Fusion 360.
  • Documented measurements and design decisions to support detector component reviews.
SLAC PCB Design Impedance Matching CAD

Undergraduate Researcher · Rubin Observatory LSST Project (2024–Present)

Investigating how Rubin LSST simulated data classifies high-magnitude, redshifted stars and galaxies as part of the CSU Stanislaus research group.

  • Built Python analysis pipelines in Jupyter Notebooks for catalog queries and validation.
  • Used Table Access Protocol (TAP) services to retrieve, cross-reference, and quality-check LSST data products.
  • Preparing a follow-up study on morphology and host galaxy identification for potential GRB sources.
Python Jupyter Table Access Protocol McNair Scholar

Research Coursework · Gaia Interpolation Study (Fall 2024)

Honors capstone project comparing interpolation methods on Gaia data to understand how modeling choices impact stellar property estimates.

  • Evaluated Gaussian Process Regression alongside linear and cubic interpolation techniques.
  • Implemented analyses in Python with Pandas, NumPy, and Matplotlib visualizations.
  • Shared results with peers to discuss trade-offs between accuracy, computation, and interpretability.
Gaia Data Gaussian Process Regression Pandas Matplotlib
